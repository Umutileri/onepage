Project Summary

Puer Management is a project management and contribution evaluation platform designed for team-based projects with clearly defined roles and responsibilities. It enables transparent tracking of tasks, progress, and individual contributions, ensuring that every participant’s effort is visible throughout the project lifecycle.

By replacing subjective judgment with structured contribution metrics and peer-based evaluation mechanisms, the platform promotes accountability, fairness, and clarity within teams. This data-driven approach supports effective collaboration in both academic and professional project environments.

Key Features of Puer Management

We bring transparency and fairness to group work, every single time.

Transparent Task Tracking

Assign roles and tasks clearly. Every member updates their status, which automatically reflects on the group progress bar.

Real-Time Progress Visualization

Instantly see the percentage of completed work for the whole group and for each individual member on the dashboard.

Flexible Peer Evaluation

Use multiple evaluation options (named, anonymous, context-based) to ensure every contribution is scored correctly and fairly.

Team Accountability

Track every team member's contributions and ensure that responsibilities are clearly defined.

Objective Evaluation

Structured scoring based on measurable contributions, eliminating ambiguity in grading and feedback.

The Data That Drives Fairness

Unlike traditional methods, Puer Management doesn't rely on guesswork. We provide tangible data points
from task completion rates to granular peer feedback ensuring every score is justified.

  • Instant accountability checks.
  • Reduces project ambiguity.
  • Objective basis for instructor grading.

Target Audience

Puer Management is designed for teams working on projects with clearly defined roles and responsibilities. It benefits anyone involved in collaborative assignments by ensuring transparency, accountability, and fair evaluation of contributions.

Students

University students working in teams on academic projects, who need clear task assignment, progress tracking, and peer evaluation.

Instructors

Professors or mentors supervising group projects, who require data-driven insight to fairly assess contributions and provide structured feedback.

Project Teams

Small to medium-sized collaborative groups outside academics, such as clubs or work-based projects, looking for organized task management and accountability.
